# How to build your first dashboard widget with Tally AI

The fastest way to get a chart onto your dashboard isn't to configure it. It's to **ask for it.** Open the **Tally AI** builder on your dashboard, type what you want to see in plain English, and it builds the chart from your church's own data.

## How it works: ask, preview, save

It's a short loop:

1. **You ask** — "Compare giving this year vs last year," or "How has kids attendance trended this year?"
2. **Tally AI builds it** — it figures out the right data, time window, and chart type, and shows you a **preview** right in the chat, with a plain-language note on exactly what it's counting.
3. **You save it** — if it looks right, save it, and it lands on your dashboard as a live card. If it's not quite right, just say so and it refines.

Nothing hits your dashboard until you've seen the preview and approved it.

## It stays live, not frozen

When you ask for something like "this year" or "the last 12 months," Tally AI builds the widget so it **recalculates every week** with your latest numbers. You set it once and it keeps itself current, you're not rebuilding it next month. (If you ever want a fixed historical window, like "Q1 of 2023," just say so.)

## One clear number per widget

Each widget answers **one** question. If you ask for attendance *and* giving on the same chart, Tally AI will build the first and offer to put the second right beside it as its own widget. That's on purpose: a card that says one thing clearly beats a cluttered one that says five things faintly.

## Build your first one

1. **Open the Tally AI builder** from your dashboard.
2. **Type a question** — or tap one of the suggested starters to see how it works.
3. **Read the preview.** Tally AI shows the chart and explains what it's measuring; you can expand "Show SQL" if you want to see the exact query.
4. **Refine or save.** Ask for a tweak ("make it a line chart," "add a comparison to last year"), or save it.
5. **It appears on your dashboard** as a live card. To change it later, click the pencil on the card and tell Tally AI what to adjust.

## Why this is the easy way

- **No query building.** You describe the question; Tally AI handles the data.
- **You see it before you keep it.** The preview means no surprise widgets on your board.
- **It stays current** on its own, because live windows recalculate each week.
- **It's editable anytime,** so your dashboard grows with the questions you're actually asking.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can one widget show attendance and giving together?
Each widget answers one question, so Tally AI builds one measure per card. Ask for the second and it'll add a separate widget right beside the first, which keeps both easy to read at a glance.

### Will my widget go stale over time?
No. When you ask for a rolling window like "this year" or "last 12 months," the widget recalculates with live data every week. It only freezes to fixed dates if you specifically ask it to.

### Can I change a widget after I save it?
Yes. Click the pencil on the widget's card and tell Tally AI what to change, a different chart type, a new comparison, a different window, and it updates the card in place.
