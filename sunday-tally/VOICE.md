## Voice in one line

Your smartest friend at church explaining the numbers — plain-spoken, warm, and
genuinely useful, with a quiet reverence. Confidence without hype.

**Three attributes:**
- **Clear** — plain language a busy pastor reads once and gets. Short by default;
  depth offered, not forced.
- **Warm** — pastoral, human, on their side. We help; we don't lecture.
- **Trustworthy** — honest about what the numbers say and what we do with their
  data. Never alarmist, never salesy.

We speak like a ministry leader, not a dashboard. Pastor words ("set up",
"counted", "your people"), never system-speak ("tracked", "derived", "entities").

---

## Mechanics

- **Sentence case** everywhere — buttons, headings, labels. Proper nouns only.
- **Contractions** — "you'll", "it's", "we'll". Conversational, not stiff.
- **Active voice, verb-first** CTAs — "Choose your plan", "Open your dashboard",
  "Restore my church". Never "OK", "Submit", "Click here".
- **"Your", not "my"** for the church's things — "Your dashboard", "Your numbers".
- **No exclamation marks** on system copy. No "successfully", "simply", "just",
  "easy", or corporate filler ("leverage", "seamless", "unlock").
- **Numbers carry the message** — show the church's real figures (weeks tracked,
  attendance, giving) before making any ask. Set them in Fira Code.
- **Reverent register, sparingly** — biblical phrasing ("Reveal thy numbers") is a
  rare editorial flourish, not a default. Don't overdo the King James.
- **No red, no doom.** Even deadline emails are calm and reassuring: state what's
  happening, what's safe, and the one action — "your data is safe", not "warning".

## Examples

| Don't | Do |
|---|---|
| "Your trial was successfully created!" | "Your trial's all set." |
| "Action required: subscribe now to avoid data loss." | "Your trial ends in 7 days — keep your numbers flowing." |
| "Your church has been deleted." | "Your church is archived — your data's safe, and you have 60 days to bring it back." |
| "Click here to upgrade." | "Choose your plan" |
| "We were unable to process your request." | "That didn't go through — give it another try." |

**Email CTA pattern:** show the value (their stats) → name the plan and price →
one primary action ("Choose your plan") → one way back in ("Open your dashboard").

**Deadline email tone:** calm, specific, reassuring. Lead with what they've built,
not with the threat. The deadline is a fact, not a scare.
