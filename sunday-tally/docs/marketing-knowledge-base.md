## What is Sunday Tally?

Sunday Tally makes church analytics simple. Track any number your church cares about and see it week over week, month over month, and year over year — without the spreadsheet.

**Mission:** Church analytics made simple.

Sunday Tally is not a church management system (ChMS). It doesn't manage member records, process giving, or handle check-ins. It's the analytics layer that works alongside your ChMS, not instead of it. If your church uses Planning Center, Breeze, Tithe.ly, or another ChMS, Sunday Tally works alongside it.

Sunday Tally is your data person. There's nothing to configure and no formulas to write — a volunteer who's never used church software can enter numbers, and Tally AI does the analysis.

## What You Can Track

Sunday Tally is not locked into a preset list. Your church defines what matters.

**The four tracking types:**

- **Attendance** — People in the room. Headcount. Averages automatically skip weeks a service didn't run, as long as you leave those weeks blank instead of entering zero.
- **Volunteers** — People serving. Add one count per role and the dashboard totals them automatically. You never create a "Total Volunteers" count — the system does the math.
- **Stats** — Everything that's a number but not a headcount. Baptisms, salvations, hands raised, first-time guests, parking cars, connection cards, food pantry visits — anything you count that doesn't fit neatly into attendance or volunteers.
- **Giving** — Dollar amounts. All your giving sources add up automatically. You never create a "Total Giving" count — same reason as volunteers.

**Common things churches track:**
Attendance (adults, kids, youth, online/livestream, first-time guests), giving (by source — app, online, cash, special fund), volunteers (by role — band, ushers, greeters, parking, kids check-in), salvations, baptisms, new members, small group counts, benevolence requests, counseling appointments, parking cars — anything your ministry counts.

**You decide your volunteer roles.** Operations, parking, band, singers, greeters — Sunday Tally tracks each role separately and totals them automatically.

**Can I track something off the list, like a food pantry?**
Yes. Add any custom metric — if your church counts it, Sunday Tally can hold it.

**Can I track small groups?**
Yes — and you can go further. Make a "Small Groups" ministry and add each group inside it. Because Sunday Tally supports ministries with subgroups, each group mirrors the same metrics you track and they all roll up automatically. You can see how every group is doing against total attendance without touching a formula.

**Can I track online or livestream attendance?**
Yes. Online or livestream viewers are just another attendance count alongside your in-room headcount.

**Can I track giving by fund or source?**
Yes. Add a separate count for each source — app, online, cash, a special fund — and they total automatically.

**Can I track baptisms and salvations?**
Yes. Those live in Stats and compare week over week and year over year like everything else.

**Can I track first-time guests?**
Yes. Sunday Tally counts how many, not who — so it won't track whether a specific guest returned. That's a job for your ChMS.

## Church Structure — Ministries, Groups, and Services

**Top-level vs. nested — what's the difference?**
Anything at the top of your structure gets its own card on the dashboard — its own color, its own numbers, it stands alone. Anything nested inside something else is a group. Groups roll their numbers up to the parent. You count them separately in Entries, but the dashboard shows one combined total for the parent.

**When do you make something top-level?**
Make it top-level when it deserves its own dashboard card — a whole service (Experience, LifeKids), something church-wide that doesn't belong to one service (Giving), or anything you want to see trending on its own over time.

**When do you nest it as a group?**
Nest it when it's a breakdown of something that already exists. You want the detail in Entries but one combined total on the dashboard.

**The scope rule — one of the most important things to get right:**
If something is counted once for the whole week, it doesn't belong under a service-specific ministry. Giving is almost always a once-per-week church-wide number — it needs its own top-level node, not under a service that runs multiple times.

**Roll-up vs. grand total:**
A roll-up combines entries within one ministry (Experience 9am + 11am → Experience total). The grand total is the headline number on the dashboard that combines across ministries — you define what it adds up in Settings → Setup → Totals.

**Blank is not zero:**
If a service didn't meet, leave it blank. Blank means the service didn't happen — Sunday Tally skips blank weeks in averages so a cancelled service doesn't drag your numbers down. Zero means it ran and nobody showed up. These are different things.

**Can I set up multiple services on the same Sunday?**
Absolutely. Track each service time separately and Sunday Tally rolls them up automatically.

**Can I track a service that only happens once a month?**
Yes. Set it as a monthly service in your schedule and Sunday Tally only expects an entry when it runs — off weeks stay blank and don't pull your averages down.

## The Dashboard

At-a-glance cards for attendance, giving, and serving (this week vs last), and a deep set of key metrics that regenerate automatically from your core numbers — attendance, volunteers, giving. You can show or hide them. Plus a totals table across current week, last 4 weeks, year-to-date, and prior year.

Yes. The standard dashboard builds itself the moment your church is set up. There's nothing to configure — add a metric and it appears. Add a ministry and its section appears.

Yes. Every metric shows current week, 4-week average, year-to-date, and prior year side by side, with percentage-change indicators.

**Does it update automatically?**
Yes. The moment numbers are entered, the dashboard recalculates — no refresh, no formulas.

**Can I see per-campus data?**
Yes. Filter the dashboard to a single campus or see all campuses rolled up together. The main church sets the metrics every campus tracks, so the numbers stay consistent across locations.

**Can I change what the grand total adds up?**
Yes. In Settings → Setup → Totals you choose what the headline number includes — attendance, volunteers, stats, giving — and whether it shows as a weekly average or a running total.

**Can we see year-over-year giving trends?**
Yes. Giving shows current week, 4-week average, year-to-date, and prior year side by side — every week, automatically.

**Can we see which Sunday had our highest attendance?**
Yes. Ask Tally AI and it pulls the answer straight from your history — no digging through spreadsheets.

**Can we track volunteer ratios automatically?**
Yes. Add your volunteer roles and attendance counts and Sunday Tally calculates the ratio automatically. A target ratio and met/not met indicator come built into the standard dashboard.

**Can Sunday Tally help us spot a declining trend before it becomes a crisis?**
Yes. That's one of the core things it's built for — week-over-week and year-over-year comparisons make a slow decline visible long before it shows up in a conversation. Tally AI can surface those patterns the moment you ask.
