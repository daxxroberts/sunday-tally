# Field & Flock — Blog Image Brand

The official illustration style for all Sunday Tally blog ("Field Notes") cover images.
A recurring cast — a small shepherd in a deep-indigo cloak and a simple flock of
rounded sheep — living in calm, textured editorial landscapes. The pastor-as-shepherd /
congregation-as-flock metaphor fits church analytics without any church cliché. Each
cover *quietly* supports its article's idea; the real charts stay inside the post.

- **Generator:** OpenAI image generation (paste the prompt below). Banana-claude is an
  alternative once installed.
- **Aspect ratio:** 16:9. **No text in the image.**
- **Save to:** `public/blog/<slug>-cover.png` (frontmatter `coverImage` / `ogImage`
  paths are already wired to these).
- **Palette:** deep indigo, charcoal blue, muted sage, stone gray, warm ivory, dusty
  sky blue, small soft gold accents.
- **Hard rule:** no crosses, steeples, stained glass, pews, halos, or religious symbols;
  no red (the brand never uses red — burnout/decline reads through mood, not alarm).

## Prompt template (constant boilerplate)

Swap only the article title line and the `<SCENE>` paragraph. Everything else stays
identical so all 15 covers feel like one illustrated world.

```
Create a modern editorial illustration in a "Field & Flock" brand style.

The image should use textured geometric editorial illustration: abstracted rolling
hills, layered flat shapes, subtle paper-like texture, strong composition, and
cinematic negative space. The mood should feel calm, mature, strategic, and refined.

Feature a small shepherd wearing a deep indigo cloak and a simple flock of rounded
sheep. The shepherd should feel observant and steady, not cute or cartoonish. The
sheep should be simplified silhouettes or soft geometric forms, used as a quiet
metaphor for church attendance, engagement, and congregation movement.

Avoid all church clichés: no crosses, steeples, stained glass, pews, halos, or
religious symbols.

Use a restrained, sophisticated color palette: deep indigo, charcoal blue, muted
sage, stone gray, warm ivory, dusty sky blue, and small soft gold accents.

The scene should visually support this article idea:
"<ARTICLE TITLE>"

<SCENE>

Style keywords: textured geometric editorial illustration, modern SaaS brand
illustration, abstract landscape, layered shapes, refined minimalism, conceptual
storytelling, matte texture, premium editorial art, calm strategic mood.

Aspect ratio: 16:9.
No text in the image.
```

## The 15 scenes (slug → title → scene)

**Analytics**

1. `church-analytics-pearsons-law` — *Church Analytics and the One Principle That Makes Ministries Grow* (HUB)
   At first light, the shepherd stands on a rise calmly taking in the whole flock spread
   across gently terraced hills. A single worn path loops from the flock back to the
   shepherd and outward again — a quiet cycle of watching, noting, and tending. The
   flock is healthy and unhurried. Foundational, strategic, the cornerstone image.

2. `attendance-up-church-shrinking` — *Your Attendance Went Up. So Why Does the Church Feel Smaller?* (FEATURED)
   A healthy-looking flock gathers in the foreground, suggesting growth on the surface.
   In the distance, a few sheep subtly drift over a far ridge, smaller and slightly
   faded. The shepherd notices them quietly. Subtle, not overly literal.

3. `summer-attendance-dip` — *The Summer Attendance Dip Is Usually a Lie* (FEATURED)
   A warm, high-sun afternoon. The field looks sparse at a glance because most of the
   flock is resting in the cool shade of a single broad tree; a couple still graze in
   the open. Nothing is wrong — they are simply out of the heat. The shepherd rests
   easy nearby. The emptiness is only apparent.

4. `kids-attendance-leading-indicator` — *Your Kids' Room Is Quietly Forecasting Your Church*
   A few small lambs at the leading edge of the flock have already turned toward a path
   over the next ridge, while the grown sheep still graze behind, unaware. The shepherd
   notices the lambs' direction. The little ones quietly forecast where the whole flock
   is heading.

5. `giving-per-person-discipleship` — *Giving Per Person Is a Discipleship Number, Not a Budget Number*
   Two gentle hollows in the same landscape, each with a single haystack of identical
   size. One hollow holds many sheep gathered around it; the other only a few. Same
   harvest, very different number of contributors. The shepherd stands between,
   considering the difference. Conceptual, not literal.

6. `volunteer-burnout-ratio` — *You Can See Volunteer Burnout Before It Happens*
   A small handful of sheep carry the whole flock's load — bundles of hay or skins of
   water — along a path, while a larger, lighter group rests behind. The late-day gold
   sun sits low. The shepherd watches the faithful few with quiet concern, ready to
   step in. Caring and early, never alarmed.

**How-To**

7. `first-time-guest-followup` — *A First-Time Guest Who Serves in 30 Days Stays. One Who Doesn't, Usually Leaves.* (FEATURED)
   A single new sheep at the edge of the flock pauses at a fork in the path. One branch
   leads inward to join the others around the watering place; the other leads away over
   the ridge. A soft gold glow marks the path inward — the second step. The shepherd
   gently inclines toward it. The choice that decides who stays.

8. `monthly-ministry-review` — *The 20-Minute Review That Changes Everything*
   In dusk's calm light, the shepherd pauses on a rise to look back over the day's
   flock — a quiet, regular moment of taking stock before deciding tomorrow's
   direction. A few small stones set on the ground suggest a simple count. Reflective,
   ritual, unhurried.

**Getting Started**

9. `start-with-one-metric` — *Start With One Number, Not a Dashboard*
   Instead of the whole flock, the shepherd attends closely to a single sheep. A faint
   worn path is just beginning to form underfoot from returning to the same spot. The
   rest of the field is open and uncluttered. The discipline of one, repeated.

10. `what-church-should-track` — *Stop Counting What Doesn't Help You Pastor*
    The shepherd's gaze passes over a scattered field of grazing sheep, but attention
    settles on just a few that matter for the day's decision; the others soften into
    the background. Choosing what is worth watching, not counting everything.

11. `spreadsheet-quietly-fails` — *Why Your Church Spreadsheet Quietly Fails You*
    Three separate fenced paddocks sit apart on the hillside, each holding a small,
    isolated group of sheep; low fences and gaps keep them from ever meeting on common
    ground. The shepherd stands outside, unable to take all three in at once. Quietly
    siloed — an architecture problem. Slightly cooler, muted light.

**Sunday Tally Setup**

12. `set-up-what-we-track` — *How to Set Up What You Track in Sunday Tally*
    The shepherd calmly lays out a simple, orderly pen — a few connected enclosures of
    different sizes, ready to receive the flock, each clearly its own space. Everything
    is tidy and prepared before the sheep arrive.

13. `set-up-services-and-schedule` — *How to Set Up Your Services and Schedule in Sunday Tally*
    The shepherd opens the gate for two arrivals of the flock at two different times of
    day — morning light and later light — the two groups merging onto one common
    pasture as a single gathering. Rhythm and schedule, combining into one clean field.

14. `build-your-first-widget` — *How to Build Your First Dashboard Widget with Tally AI*
    The shepherd raises a simple hand-built marker or cairn on a rise — a small,
    purpose-made vantage point — from which the whole flock becomes easy to take in at
    a glance. A faint gold accent marks the new vantage. Building one clear view.

15. `import-your-history` — *How to Import Your Church's History from a Spreadsheet*
    A long, established trail of the flock's earlier footprints winds in from the far
    distance and joins the present pasture, so the day begins already full of history
    rather than empty. A small gate marks where the arriving line is welcomed in — a
    quiet review point before they enter.

**Faith / reflective piece**

16. `what-a-church-tends-to-grow` — *What a Church Tends to Grow* (the scriptural spine of the brand)
    The shepherd kneels to tend a young tree taking root beside a quiet stream, its
    first leaves opening in soft gold light, while the flock grazes peacefully across
    flourishing green hills around it. What is being tended is visibly growing —
    patient, faithful increase (Psalm 1's tree planted by flowing streams). Hopeful and
    calm. Subtle, not literal.
