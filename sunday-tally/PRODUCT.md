# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Church staff, executive pastors, and ministry leaders who need to track and analyze attendance, volunteer, and giving data but are currently trapped in complex spreadsheets or rigid, pre-packaged church management software reports.

## Product Purpose
SundayTally replaces manual spreadsheet math with intelligent, AI-driven analytics. It allows churches to import years of history instantly, ask complex questions in plain English, and generate custom dashboards on command. Success means a pastor can instantly know the health of their church without waiting for an admin to build a report.

## Brand Personality
Expert, confident, clean, and restrained. The aesthetic is heavily inspired by Northgarden—an editorial, minimalist approach using warm off-whites (`#FAFAFA`), slate/stone dark text (`stone-900`), and thin borders. It feels engineered and high-craft, avoiding the typical bubbly or overly playful SaaS look. 

## Anti-references
- "Apple Dark Mode" or overly moody tech styling
- The standard generic "Bento Box" SaaS template with repeating, identical cards and generic icons
- "Magic" AI aesthetics (sparkles, purple/pink gradients). We prefer "This is just good engineering."
- Light gray text on off-white backgrounds (poor contrast)

## Design Principles
- **Data over Decoration:** The numbers are the heroes. The UI should step back and let the insights speak.
- **Human-Readable:** Avoid overly complex data-viz when a simple, bold number or a plain-english AI summary does the job better.
- **Engineered, Not Magic:** Motion and UI flourishes should feel grounded, precise, and technical (e.g. particle networks, clean grid lines), rather than ethereal or magical.
- **Confident Restraint:** Rely on typography, generous spacing, and strict alignment rather than relying on drop-shadows and glassmorphism.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- Needs excellent contrast, as church staff users skew older. 
- Motion (like the canvas backgrounds) should be subtle and smooth, avoiding anything jarring or overly fast.
